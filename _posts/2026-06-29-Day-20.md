what_i_learned: |
  Today I learned what it looks like to trust my preparation and my practice and not think of a presentation so intensely. Usually, I get a lot of anxiety, and you can hear my breathing and nervousness in my voice. I was definitely nervous while presenting today, but something important I learned from my peers is that people are just people. The goal is to simply speak to them by projecting my voice, being clear, and being concise.

  I also learned to trust my intelligence more. I have studied enough and learned enough to speak about my project. Even though there were a few times where I feel like I could have articulated my answers to questions better, I still think I did pretty well during the midterm symposium. It showed me that I am more prepared than I sometimes give myself credit for.

blockers: |
  There were no blockers today. It was a good day overall. It was cold inside, hot outside, and we were even let out early, so I am very grateful for that.

reflection: |
  My reflection is that I can now see what I need to work on for the final symposium. I want to be more natural, more confident, and more fluent in what I have to say. I also want to feel a little more prepared when it comes to answering questions.

  I really appreciated the feedback from my peers. They especially liked the engagement questions that I started with, which made me feel good because I wanted the presentation to feel interactive and not just like I was reading information. Overall, I am proud of how I did, and I am looking forward to doing better and bigger things moving forward.
