I also learned more about my own strengths when it comes to presentation skills. I realized that I am actually pretty good at sharing information that I may not have felt fully confident about at first, especially once I take time to memorize it and practice what I want to say.
  I also learned that I am good at giving constructive criticism. I received feedback from other teams that they appreciated how I broke down what they needed to improve on in a way that did not make them feel bad. Instead, it helped them understand how they could grow and what they could do better next time. That made me feel good because I want to be someone who can help others improve while still being respectful.
blockers: |
  My blocker was consistently working on how I articulate my information and how concise I can be. During our group practice, we were able to get close to the required time, but when we presented again, we still went over by about one minute. That is better than usual, but I know I still need to practice a little more with timing. At least now I know what I want to say, so the next step is making sure I can say it clearly and within the time limit.
reflection: |
  My reflection is that after looking at the other groups, I can see how our presentation could be more visually appealing. I want to add more visuals, images, and slides that clearly show the audience what I am talking about instead of only having large words on the screen. I think adding stronger visuals will help the audience better understand the information and make the presentation feel more engaging. Overall, I feel like I am improving as a presenter, and I am starting to see what I can do to make both my speaking and my slides stronger.
