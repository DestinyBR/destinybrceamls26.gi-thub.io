what_i_learned: |
   Today I learned the importance of using AI tools to my advantage in a healthy, ethical, and intentional way. Especially when completing a literature review.
   I learned that AI can help compare ideas, organize research, and evaluate different sources. It is still my responsibility to understand the work, check the information,
   and explain my own process clearly. I also learned that when I write my overall review and paper on Alzheimer’s disease drug discovery, I can include how I used multiple
   AI tools, what prompts I used, and how I compared the different responses to strengthen my methodology. Another major thing I learned is that the words I use matter a lot. 
   Especially in computer science, machine learning, and any field where I am presenting myself as knowledgeable on a topic. Every word can be questioned, 
   so I have to be more intentional, specific, and confident when explaining my work. I also learned more about how to search for datasets for building another QSAR model, 
   while understanding that the dataset itself has to connect to compounds, Alzheimer’s disease, brain-related diseases, and the type of subjects being studied. I also 
   learned that breaks are important because they help me reset my brain and come back to the work with more focus and energy.
