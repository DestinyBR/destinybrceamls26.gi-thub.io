what_i_learned: |
  Today I learned the importance of not only being able to write code, but also being able to fluently explain my code to my teammates 
  and mentors. In a way that makes the purpose of my machine learning clear. I learned how a confusion matrix helps justify my work
  by showing how well my QSAR model is making predictions. Especially when looking at accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-scores. 
  I also gained a stronger understanding of the full model workflow. Starting with building a compound list, researching how those compounds
  connect to Alzheimer’s disease and drug discovery. Cleaning the data, checking for missing values, 
  and then using the model to study patterns between chemical structures, activities, and relationships.

  on information it has not seen before. This helped me realize that model evaluation is not just a final step, 
  but one of the most important ways to explain why we trust a certain model and why we may choose one model over another.
