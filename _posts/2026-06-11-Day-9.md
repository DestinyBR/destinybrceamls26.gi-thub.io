What I Learned Today:
Today I learned how to better explain the most important parts of a QSAR model. Along with why we are using it for 
Alzheimer’s drug discovery. I understand that QSAR helps us compare the chemical structure of compounds. Like SMILES,
with their biological activity and how they may affect the brain. I also learned how model evaluation metrics,
like accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score help us understand how well our machine learning model is performing.
Along with that, I learned how to research, analyze, and find a dataset specific to Alzheimer’s drug discovery.
I was able to find a dataset with important QSAR foundation features like compound name, SMILES, LogP, 
and other chemical properties. I download it as a CSV file, and shared it with my team so we can move closer 
to building our full QSAR model.
