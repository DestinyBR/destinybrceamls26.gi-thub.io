what_i_learned: |
  Today I learned more about what it takes to complete the docking step of our project. I learned that we need to record the best docking score for every compound, compare each compound with the reference ligand, examine the best poses, and look closely at important protein interactions. I also learned that docking results cannot stand alone. They need to be combined with ADMET, toxicity, BBB, P-gp, QSAR, and ChemBERTa information 
  so we can better understand which compounds are actually the most promising. 
  I also learned what else needs to be done for us to fully complete our final project. At this point, 
  it is really just the final steps of docking and continuing to write and complete our paper, which we would like to eventually publish. 
  I already completed my rough draft, so I feel good about having my part started and ready to keep improving.
  
  I also learned more about the tools needed for the docking workflow. I learned how RDKit can be used for 3D ligand generation, how Meeko is used for PDBQT preparation, and how AutoDock Vina is used for docking. This helped me understand that docking is not just one simple step,
  but a full process where each tool helps prepare the compound and protein for the next stage.
  
  I also learned the importance of having a circle of people who are willing to help you grow by giving real feedback. It helps me improve, look as polished as I want to and see what I need to do better. I also learned more about what activities my cohort is interested in, 
  and I am looking forward to helping make those activities fun and exciting for everyone.
