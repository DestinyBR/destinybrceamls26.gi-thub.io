what_i_learned: |
  Today I learned what it takes to complete the final steps of our docking workflow. After preparing the receptor and ligands, 
  I run AutoDock Vina in Google colab using the prepared AChE receptor, the ligand PDBQT files, and the grid center based on the donepezil binding site. 
  I also learned that the box size matters because it helps define the area where docking will take place.
  
  I learned that AutoDock Vina gives important outputs such as the docking score, docked pose file, and log file. The main output from this is the Docking Results csv, which is an organized record of the docking results for each compound.
  
  Lastly, I learned that after Vina works, we may later repeat or rescore the top compounds with another tool such as GNINA. This would help us compare rankings across tools and eventually create a consensus docking table. This step showed me that docking is not just about getting one result, but about checking, comparing, and explaining the strongest compounds in the best way possible.
