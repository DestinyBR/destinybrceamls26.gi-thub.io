what_i_learned: |
 Today I learned more about the potential I have to become a product manager and how I could use that potential to help my MSU Computer Science organization, SACS. I learned that some of the skills I already have are very valuable in the recruitment world right now, especially cross-collaboration, articulation, problem-solving, and being able to connect back to the actual problem that needs to be solved.
 
 I also made sure to ask meaningful questions, such as what makes a student stand out when many resumes have similar accomplishments. That helped me think more about how I can separate myself through how I communicate, network, and explain my experiences.
 
 I also learned that I probably should not bring my computer to events like this next time. These conventions are mostly about networking, so my iPad would have been enough. Carrying a heavy bag all day definitely made my body ache more than it needed to.
 
blockers: |
 There were no real blockers today. Next time, I want to plan out more how I would like to engage with each booth and workshop more intentionally so I can get even more out of the experience and have more useful information to share with my peers. The only small issue was that I was starving by the end of the day. I trusted the free AWS food, but honestly, a Chick-fil-A meal would have made me feel much better.
 
reflection: |
 My reflection is that this was a nice field trip where I got to connect with great individuals, visit useful booths, and get some great swag. My goal now is to learn from every compliment, piece of advice, and thought that was shared with me today. I also want to connect with everyone whose LinkedIn I received and continue building those relationships.
 
 Most importantly, I want to find ways for my SACS members to benefit from experiences like this. I want to bring back useful opportunities, advice, and connections so that more students in our organization can grow, network, and see themselves in spaces like this too.
